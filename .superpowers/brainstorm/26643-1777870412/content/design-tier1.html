Design — Section 3 of 4: Tier 1 Components

Exactly what gets built in week 1. Nothing more, nothing less.

COMPONENT 1
Login / Auth
Simple password-protected login page at app.f10strategy.com. Only Efton (and eventually team members) can access. Uses NextAuth or a hardcoded env-var password to start — no database accounts needed yet.

Why simple: You do not need user management in Tier 1. One password. One tool. Upgrade to Supabase auth in Tier 2 when reps need their own logins.
COMPONENT 2
Lead Pipeline View
A simple table or kanban board showing all leads by status: New → Reviewed → Demoed → Proposal Sent → Closed → Active.

Click any lead to open their full profile. Update status with one click. Notes field for anything Efton wants to remember about the prospect. Saves to Supabase.
COMPONENT 3
Client Records
When a lead is marked Closed, a client record is created. Tracks: business name, contact info, SKU (DFY/DWY), payment status, provisioning status, go-live date, monthly metrics.

This is Efton's source of truth for every active client. Who is live, what they are paying, and when their next check-in is.
COMPONENT 4
Subdomain + Domain
The tool moves from its current Vercel preview URL to app.f10strategy.com. This requires pointing the subdomain in Cloudflare or wherever f10strategy.com DNS lives, and adding a custom domain in Vercel.

One-time setup. Takes about 20 minutes. Makes the tool look production-ready immediately.
What is intentionally NOT in Tier 1
❌ Stripe billing (Tier 2)
❌ Client-facing portal (Tier 2)
❌ Multi-rep logins (Tier 2)
❌ White label layer (Tier 3)
❌ Agency dashboard (Tier 3)
❌ Automated provisioning (Tier 3)

Does this look right? Four components. Login, pipeline view, client records, and the subdomain. That is the entire Tier 1 build. Everything else waits until clients tell you what they need next.
