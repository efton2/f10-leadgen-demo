Advisory Council — Delivery Testing Verdict

How do you make sure it works before a paying client depends on it?

Option A — Test Internally First
Right instinct. Wrong target.

Your own brands will not generate the edge cases a real SMB client will. The stakes and usage patterns are different. You will get false confidence.

Verdict: Use only if provisioning is completely untested.
Option B — Paid Beta Client
Introduces friction at the worst time. Discounting undermines premium positioning right when you are trying to establish it.

"Beta pricing" signals lack of confidence before you have even started.

Verdict: Skip. Do not discount. Do not label it beta.
COUNCIL PICK
Option C — Manual Delivery First
Charge full price. Deliver manually. Document every step.

The manual delivery IS the quality guarantee. Efton personally handling it is stronger than any SLA.

Verdict: This is exactly what Zapier, Intercom, and Drift did.
The Concierge MVP Pattern

Zapier launched by manually connecting APIs for early users before a single automation was built. Intercom's founders wrote every customer message by hand before building the inbox. Drift signed enterprise deals and fulfilled them with a Google Doc and a Slack channel.

The pattern is always the same: sell the outcome, deliver it manually, automate the parts that break repeatedly.

The Three Rules for Manual Delivery

RULE 1 — CHARGE FULL PRICE
The client is buying the outcome, not the automation. Efton personally delivering it is a stronger guarantee than any contract. Never call it a beta. Never discount it.
RULE 2 — DOCUMENT EVERY STEP
Every manual step during clients 1–5 gets written down as a runbook. Every step Efton performs is a future n8n node. The runbook IS the automation blueprint.
RULE 3 — AUTOMATE ON REPEATABILITY
Do not automate on a timeline. Automate when the runbook is stable across 3 clients with no deviation. Proven repeatability, not a calendar date, is the trigger.

Bottom line: The first client is not a test. It is the product. The automation is what gets built from what the product reveals. Your concern about testing is valid — the answer is that you are the test. And you are the best quality guarantee a first client can get.
