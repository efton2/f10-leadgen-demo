One Brand or Two?

F10 Strategy is already live and positioned. Where does the production platform live?

What we just saw on f10strategy.com: "Stop Losing Leads You Already Paid For" — F10 Strategy installs AI-powered follow-up systems. Already live, already branded, already client-facing. The question is how the production platform relates to it.

A

Platform Lives Inside F10 Strategy

The production tool is the delivery engine behind f10strategy.com. Clients sign a contract, you provision them at app.f10strategy.com. The platform is F10 Strategy's technology — not a separate product. White label happens by rebranding the whole thing.

One brand. Simpler. F10 Strategy IS the product.

B

AOS Is a Separate Product

AI Operator Systems launches as its own brand and product. F10 Strategy becomes the first customer of AOS — proof it works. AOS is what you sell to agencies for white-label. It has its own domain, its own login, its own identity.

Two brands. More scalable. AOS can outlive any single client.

C

F10 Strategy Is the Flagship — Platform Sells Separately

F10 Strategy is the proof-of-concept case study. The platform is sold separately as AOS to agencies and consultants who want to offer the same system under their own brand. F10 Strategy's results become the sales pitch for AOS.

Best of both. F10 Strategy builds trust, AOS builds recurring revenue.

My recommendation: Option C. F10 Strategy already has positioning, a live site, and a story — use it as the flagship case study. AOS becomes the platform product sold to agencies. The client-facing brand (F10 Strategy) and the platform product (AOS) serve different buyers and both generate revenue.
