What to Build — and In What Order

The demo works. The question is what the production platform needs before you can take your first paying client.

What the demo already does: Search businesses → pull real data → live AI voice demo → send branded proposal → ACE follows up.
What it is missing for production: Login/auth, lead tracking (CRM), client provisioning, billing, client portal, multi-rep support, white label layer.

A

Lean MVP — Get Paid First

Add only what you need to close and service your first 5 DFY clients: password protection, a simple lead pipeline view, and manual client provisioning. No billing system, no client portal, no white label. You handle everything manually while you learn what clients actually need.

Build time: 1 week  |  Revenue: starts immediately  |  Risk: lowest

B

Full Production Build

Build the complete platform in one shot: auth, CRM, Stripe billing, client portal, multi-rep support, white label layer, Revenue Rescue integration. Everything at once. Solid product, but 6–8 weeks before you close your first paying client.

Build time: 6–8 weeks  |  Revenue: delayed  |  Risk: highest (building without client feedback)

C

Sequenced — Build Each Tier as You Need It

Tier 1 (internal tool with auth + CRM) → close first DFY clients → Tier 2 (multi-rep + billing) → close DWY clients → Tier 3 (white label) → close agency deals. Each tier is revenue-generating before the next one starts. You never build what you do not yet need.

Build time: 1–2 weeks per tier  |  Revenue: starts week 2  |  Risk: lowest overall

My recommendation: Option C. The sequenced build matches exactly what the advisory council said about not building complexity before you have proof. Tier 1 is live in a week and you can walk into a dentist's office the following Monday. Everything you learn from those first clients shapes what you build in Tier 2.
